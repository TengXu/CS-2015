The Holocaust
The Holocaust, sometimes called The Shoah (Hebrew: השואה), was when Nazi Germany killed people in a planned and forced way during World War II. Approximately eleven million peoples were killed, including Jews, gypsies, the disabled, Slavs, homosexuals, bisexuals, transgenders, communists and Jehovah's Witnesses). Many were rounded up, put in ghettos, forced to work in concentration camps and then killed in big gas chambers.
